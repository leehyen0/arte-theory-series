# Publication Strategy

## Current stage

Public theory-first v0.1.

The goal is feedback, not a claim of validation.

## Recommended order

1. Keep all five repositories public.
2. Publish this index repository.
3. Share a short post asking for feedback.
4. Build v0.2 toy experiments.
5. Only then consider GitHub releases.
6. Only after stable toy artifacts consider Zenodo DOI.
7. Only after stronger manuscript fit consider arXiv.

## Why not arXiv immediately?

The series is currently theory-first and early. A premature arXiv submission can make the work look overclaimed if toy artifacts and category fit are not clear.

## Best immediate feedback target

Ask for feedback on:

- the five-paper chain,
- whether the definitions are clear,
- which related work is missing,
- whether the claim boundary is strong enough,
- which toy experiment would best test the sequence.

## Recommended title for public post

"Bias selects; complementary axes preserve; questions perturb search: a theory-first draft series"

## Recommended short tagline

A theory-first series on how agentic systems might select, preserve alternatives, integrate time, ask better questions, and test candidate laws without claiming AGI or consciousness.
