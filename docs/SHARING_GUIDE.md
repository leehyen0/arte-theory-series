# Sharing Guide

## Safe one-sentence description

A five-paper theory-first series on selection, preserved unrealized alternatives, temporal integration, question-induced search, and candidate-law testing.

## Safe English post

I published a five-part theory-first draft series on cognitive and agentic structure:

1. Bias as Controlled Asymmetry
2. Complementary Possibility Axes
3. Temporal Superposition and Self-Transcendent Metacognition
4. QuestionField Theory
5. LawGenesis Theory

Core chain: bias selects; complementary axes preserve unrealized alternatives; temporal superposition integrates past evidence, present execution, preserved alternatives, and possible future states; QuestionField determines what should be actively asked next; LawGenesis tests candidate patterns through counterexample pressure, verification gates, and later consumption traces.

This is an early public draft series. It does not claim AGI, superintelligence, consciousness, sentience, paranormal precognition, literal quantum cognition/consciousness, model-weight modification, public/offbox proof, official benchmark validation, validated experimental results, externally reproduced performance improvement, safety certification, or deployment readiness. Feedback on framing, formalization, and toy experiment design is welcome.

## Where to share

- GitHub profile README
- LessWrong shortform or draft post
- Substack
- X / Twitter

## What not to say

Do not say:

- "This proves AGI."
- "This is validated superintelligence."
- "This proves consciousness."
- "This is quantum consciousness."
- "This modifies model weights."
- "This is public/offbox proof."
- "This has externally reproduced results."
- "This is safety certified."
- "This is deployment ready."
- "The internal logs are public proof."

## What to ask for

Ask for:

- terminology feedback,
- related work suggestions,
- formalization critique,
- toy experiment suggestions,
- claim-boundary critique.
