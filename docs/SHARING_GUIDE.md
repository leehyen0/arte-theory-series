# Sharing Guide

## Safe one-sentence description

A five-paper theory-first series on selection, preserved unrealized alternatives, temporal integration, question-induced search, and candidate-law testing.

## Safe English post

I published a five-part theory-first draft series on cognitive and agentic structure:

1. Bias as Controlled Asymmetry
2. Complementary Possibility Axes
3. Temporal Superposition and Self-Transcendent Metacognition
4. QuestionField Theory
5. LawGenesis Theory

Core chain: bias selects; complementary axes preserve unrealized alternatives; temporal superposition integrates past evidence, present execution, preserved alternatives, and possible future states; QuestionField determines what should be actively asked next; LawGenesis tests candidate patterns through counterexample pressure and later consumption traces.

This is an early public draft series. It does not claim AGI, superintelligence, consciousness, literal quantum cognition/consciousness, official benchmark validation, or validated experimental results. Feedback on framing, formalization, and toy experiment design is welcome.

## Where to share

- GitHub profile README
- LessWrong shortform or draft post
- Substack

## What not to say

Do not say:

- "This proves AGI."
- "This is validated superintelligence."
- "This proves consciousness."
- "This is quantum consciousness."
- "This has externally reproduced results."
- "The internal logs are public proof."

## What to ask for

Ask for:

- terminology feedback,
- related work suggestions,
- formalization critique,
- toy experiment suggestions,
- claim-boundary critique.
